## Navigation Guide

### Investigate

- **Investigations** are the primary reasoning threads.
- **Articles** are modular supporting notes, evidence, and summaries.
- **Scratchpads** are disposable freehand pages for raw thinking before anything enters the formal system.

### Map

- **Areas** are broad domains.
- **Topics** are focused subjects within domains.
- **Tags** are lightweight cross-cutting labels.
- **Questions** are inquiry prompts that drive investigations.

### Manage

- **Admin** opens the content management interface.
- **Publish** opens the deployment workflow page.

### Search

Use Search to quickly find entries across investigations, articles, scratchpads, and questions. Search works on title, body content, tags, areas, topics, and associated questions.

## Terminology

### Investigation

A long-form reasoning unit with narrative body, context (areas/topics/tags), metadata, and lineage.

### Article

A reusable support artifact linked to one or more investigations.

### Scratchpad

A low-ceremony page for raw, discardable thought that stays outside the formal investigation and article workflow.

### Lineage

Connections from an entry to related items (for example attached articles and related investigations).

### Context Strip

The row below the title containing key context chips such as areas, topics, tags, and attached investigations.

## Reading Detail Pages

1. Title row gives the entry name.
2. Context row gives classification chips.
3. Left column contains the full narrative body.
4. Right column has metadata and lineage panels.

## Tips

- Use tags for temporary labels and promote stable ones to topics.
- Keep investigation bodies narrative-first; avoid replacing them with link lists.
- Use Questions to anchor scope and intent.
